French Apple cake

(AKA the 3/4 Recipe)

This great cake has a ton of apples and a modest amount of sugar. Let the apples sweeten up the cake for you! This is so good. Don't add cinnamon, it wouldn't be French.

ingredients

  • ¾ cup white sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • ¾ tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 3 tablespoons rum
  • ¾ stick butter, softened, divided
  • ¾ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¾ teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 4 cups chopped peeled apples, or more taste

steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Butter a 9-inch springform pan.
  2. Combine sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract in a bowl; beat with an electric mixer until smooth. Mix in rum. Mix in 1/2 of the butter.
  3. Combine flour, baking powder, and salt together in a separate bowl. Add 1/2 of the flour mixture to the egg mixture; mix well. Mix in remaining butter and flour. Fold in apples.
  4. Scrape batter into the prepared pan. Smooth the top gently.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean, 50 to 60 minutes.
